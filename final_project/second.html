Where to Start When Gardening

Gardening can be intimidating at first but there are a lot of useful websites and videos that can help you get started on switching to a home grown vegetable diet. The first thing you should do is find the location you want to start which can be inside your home, outside in the yard, or in a greenhouse. Next you want to set up a watering system or plan on when you should water your plants. After that, you want to look into your gardening zone to see what produce grows best in your zone by state. This will determine what soil, tools, and seeds to buy for your garden set up. In the beginning you will want to be in your garden as much as possible making sure you are watering them correctly and preventing bugs from terrorizing your new plants, but after awhile you will be a natural.

Tools and Supplies

  • Gloves
  • Pruning Sheers
  • Loopers
  • Garden Fork
  • Hand Trowel
  • Hoe
  • Watering can or hose

Gardening outside/greenhouse

There are so many different ways to garden nowadays and two of the most common are outside in a garden box or in a greenhouse. Gardening outside is beneficial for the plants as some plants thrive off the climate and direct sunlight. Also, plants outside do not need to be watered as much as they can last off of rain water as well as when you water them. The benefit to using a greenhouse for gardening produce is that you can control the climate and what comes in and out. Having plants in a greenhouse can make it to where you can still get the needed sunlight without bugs or the climate disturbing your plants that may not grow as well in your zone. Whichever you prefer doing your research and planning for anything will be beneficial in the long run.

Tips and Tricks

  • Choose high yield plants
  • Grow your plants high
  • Check your soil and sun for each plant
  • Research healthy ways to repel bugs
  • Do not over water
  • Grow what you love
